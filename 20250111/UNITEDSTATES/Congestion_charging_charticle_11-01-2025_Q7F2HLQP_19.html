Congestion pricing in New York

Congestion pricing works

AFTER DECADES of false starts, legal challenges and stalling, the first congestion-pricing scheme in America has the green light. Joshua and Benjamin Moshes, students at Northeastern University and Brown University respectively, are monitoring commuting times. Their tracker collects traffic data from Google Maps every 15 minutes across 19 routes. Early results are encouraging. The time to drive through the Holland Tunnel was cut by half during rush hour, from an average of 23 minutes on most Monday mornings, to just 12 on January 6th. The time spent crossing the East River via the Queensboro Bridge was also halved.
